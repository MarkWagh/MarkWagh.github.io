📄   Research Paper
Analysis of EEG Signals to Predict 'Negative Emotions' using OpenBCI

Psychological changes in humans are the result of emotions which occur due to activities in daily life. To understand these changes in behavioral pattern, research on parameters like EEG, blood pressure, temperature of the body, and psychological parameters like Perceived Stress Scale (PSS) has emerged. Emotions are an integral part of our daily lives — based on which an investigation has been made to predict the impact of negative emotions (stress) using Electroencephalogram (EEG) and blood pressure. Collection of EEG signals using the 8-channel OpenBCI device and understanding the pattern of the data collected is performed. A statistical analysis and mathematical representation depicting the relationship among the parameters is developed. The performance of classification algorithms — SVM, KNN, and Decision Tree — on the data collected is compared. Prediction of stress using EEG and BP can help psychiatrists and neuro-specialists to perform quick analysis of subjects.
